What is Red Tides?

A red tide is the discoloration of water. Factors that influence red tides include warm ocean temperatures, low salinity, high nutrients in the ocean. Red tides is basically eutrophication in ocean. Red tides cause high loss of revenue as they cause fish to be be poisionous as the fish go thorugh biomagnemfication. Red tides are made up of different type of algae. They are called Dinoflagellates. Eutrophication and Red tides are the same except that Red tide only occurs in ocean. The dinoflagellates that cause these are really small and can only be looked at from a microscope.
