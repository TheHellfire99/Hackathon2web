What causes Red Tides?

Factors affecting red tides include excess nutrients being dumped into the ocean, warm ocean temperatures, low salinity, and calm seas. Red tides can spread with the help of winds, water currents, storms, or vessels like ships. Red tides are caused when Dinoflagellates rapidly reproduce and pile on the shores of states. Due to these organisms people suffer from posioning. They also cause very low ph in ocean. Red tides caused the same way Eutrophication is caused in lakes and ponds.
